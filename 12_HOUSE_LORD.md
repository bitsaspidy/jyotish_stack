Lord of every house in 12 houses

1st House Lord (Lagna Lord) in All 12 Houses
English: The 1st house lord, or Lagna Lord, is the most critical planet in a chart, representing
the self, physical body, personality, health, and overall life direction. Its placement indicates
where the native’s vitality and identity are focused.


1. 1st House:
○ English: When the 1st house lord is in the 1st house, it creates a powerful
alignment of self-awareness and vitality. The native possesses a strong
personality, good health (unless afflicted), and a clear sense of identity. They are
self-reliant, confident, and often take initiative in life. This placement emphasizes
personal growth and independence, making the individual a natural leader. Their
life revolves around self-expression, and they tend to be proactive in shaping
their destiny. Health is generally robust unless malefic influences like Rahu or
Saturn weaken the lord.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: The Sun is the natural karaka (significator) of the
1st house (self and vitality). If the Sun is the Lagna Lord and placed here,
it enhances ego and leadership but, if afflicted (e.g., combust or with
malefics), may lead to arrogance, selfishness, or health issues like
headaches or heart problems.


2. 2nd House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 2nd house connects the self to wealth, family,
and speech. The native’s identity and efforts are directed toward accumulating

wealth and maintaining family harmony. They are likely to have a pleasant voice,
good financial acumen, and a personality shaped by material security. Health
may depend on financial stability, and they work hard to support their loved ones.
This placement often indicates a practical individual who uses their vitality to
build resources, with family playing a significant role in their life’s direction.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Jupiter, the karaka of wealth and family, if placed
here as the 1st lord, enhances prosperity but, if afflicted (e.g., with Rahu
or Saturn), may cause family disputes or overemphasis on money,
neglecting emotional bonds, potentially leading to stress-related health
issues like throat or digestive problems.


3. 3rd House:
○ English: With the 1st house lord in the 3rd house, the native’s identity is tied to
courage, communication, and siblings. They are bold, skillful, and often excel in
fields requiring initiative, such as writing, media, or entrepreneurship. Siblings or
short travels play a significant role in shaping their life. This placement gives a
dynamic, energetic personality focused on self-expression. The native thrives on
challenges, using their vitality to master skills and assert themselves, though they
may need to guard against overexertion impacting their health.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Mars, the karaka of courage and siblings, as the
1st lord here strengthens determination but, if afflicted (e.g., with Ketu),
may lead to conflicts with siblings or impulsive behavior affecting health
(e.g., accidents, nerve issues).

4. 4th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 4th house ties the self to home, mother, and
emotional security. The native is deeply attached to their roots, finding comfort in
domestic life and property matters. They may have a nurturing personality and
good health if supported by a stable home environment. Their identity is shaped
by familial bonds and a sense of belonging, often making them the emotional
anchor of the family. Property or land-related pursuits may define their life’s
focus.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: The Moon, karaka of the 4th (mother and
emotions), as the 1st lord here enhances emotional depth but, if afflicted
(e.g., with Saturn), may lead to mood swings or domestic unrest,
impacting health (e.g., chest or stomach issues).


5. 5th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 5th house connects the self to creativity,
intelligence, children, and romance. The native is imaginative, intellectually
sharp, and often blessed with progeny. Their identity shines through creative
pursuits (e.g., arts, education) or speculative ventures, where they take risks with
confidence. This placement fosters a youthful, playful personality, and they derive
vitality from intellectual or romantic engagements. Children or students may play
a key role in their life’s purpose.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Jupiter, karaka of children and wisdom, as the
1st lord here boosts intellect and progeny but, if afflicted (e.g., with Rahu),
may delay children or lead to overconfidence in speculation, affecting
health (e.g., stress, heart issues).

6. 6th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 6th house ties the self to competition, service,
and overcoming obstacles. The native is hardworking, resilient, and thrives in
challenging environments. They may excel in service-oriented fields (e.g.,
healthcare, law) or defeat enemies through determination. Health may be a
concern, requiring effort to maintain, as their vitality is often tested by stress or
conflicts. This placement gives a fighter’s spirit, with the native channeling their
energy into problem-solving.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Mars, karaka of enemies and competition, as the
1st lord here enhances fighting spirit but, if afflicted (e.g., with Saturn),
may cause chronic health issues (e.g., injuries, inflammation) or
excessive stress from conflicts.


7. 7th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 7th house connects the self to partnerships,
marriage, and public dealings. The native’s identity is heavily influenced by their
spouse or business partners, and they thrive in collaborative environments. They
are charming, diplomatic, and often successful in relationships or trade. Health
may be linked to the spouse’s well-being, and their vitality shines in social
interactions. This placement often indicates a life shaped by others, with the
native finding purpose through alliances.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Venus, karaka of spouse and partnerships, as
the 1st lord here strengthens relational harmony but, if afflicted (e.g., with
Mars), may lead to conflicts in marriage or over-dependence, affecting
health (e.g., kidney or reproductive issues).

8. 8th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 8th house ties the self to transformation,
occult, and longevity. The native is introspective, resilient, and drawn to mysteries
(e.g., astrology, research). Their life involves sudden changes, and they may face
health challenges or hidden struggles. This placement gives depth and a
secretive personality, with vitality directed toward understanding life’s deeper
truths. They often emerge stronger from crises, though longevity may be a
concern if afflicted.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Saturn, karaka of longevity, as the 1st lord here
strengthens endurance but, if afflicted (e.g., with Rahu), may reduce life
span or cause chronic illnesses (e.g., joint pain, fatigue).

9. 9th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 9th house connects the self to luck, spirituality,
and higher learning. The native is fortunate, wise, and guided by a strong moral
compass. They may excel in teaching, philosophy, or travel-related fields, with
their identity shaped by faith and a quest for knowledge. Health is generally
robust due to optimism, and their vitality is expressed through exploration and
righteousness. This placement often indicates a blessed life with a higher
purpose.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Jupiter, karaka of luck and wisdom, as the 1st
lord here amplifies fortune but, if afflicted (e.g., with Ketu), may lead to
over-idealism or detachment, affecting physical vitality (e.g., liver or
metabolic issues).

10. 10th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 10th house ties the self to career, status, and
public life. The native is ambitious, authoritative, and achieves recognition
through their efforts. Their identity is defined by professional success, and they
often hold leadership roles. Health is linked to work stress or reputation, with
vitality channeled into achieving prominence. This placement indicates a life
focused on worldly achievements, with the native’s personality shining in the
public sphere.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Saturn, karaka of career, as the 1st lord here
strengthens discipline but, if afflicted (e.g., with Mars), may delay success
or cause overwork-related health issues (e.g., back pain, fatigue).

11. 11th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 11th house connects the self to gains,
ambitions, and social networks. The native is goal-oriented, socially active, and
achieves desires through teamwork or friendships. Their identity is tied to fulfilling
aspirations, and they enjoy a wide circle of influence. Health benefits from
optimism and support, with vitality expressed through collective endeavors. This
placement often brings a life of abundance and social fulfillment.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Jupiter, karaka of gains, as the 1st lord here
boosts prosperity but, if afflicted (e.g., with Rahu), may lead to
over-ambition or strained friendships, affecting mental peace or health
(e.g., anxiety).


12. 12th House:
○ English: The 1st house lord in the 12th house ties the self to spirituality, isolation,
or foreign lands. The native may be introspective, seeking liberation or living

abroad. Their identity is shaped by detachment, and they may face losses or
health challenges requiring solitude for healing. This placement gives a mystical
or reclusive personality, with vitality directed toward inner growth or distant
pursuits. They often find peace away from worldly chaos.
■ Karko Bhava Nashyati: Saturn, karaka of losses and isolation, as the 1st
lord here enhances detachment but, if afflicted (e.g., with Ketu), may
increase isolation or cause health issues (e.g., insomnia, depression).
